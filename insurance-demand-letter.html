Insurance Demand Letter

What This Situation Means

An insurance demand letter is formal correspondence requesting specific settlement payment based on documented loss value. Demand letters typically follow denial, underpayment, or prolonged delay, and serve to clearly state the policyholder's position and requested resolution before pursuing further remedies.

Demand letters should include specific payment amount requested, factual basis for the demand, supporting documentation, and reasonable deadline for response. Demands should be factual and professional, not threatening or emotional.

Why Insurers Do This

Demand letters may be necessary due to:

  • Inadequate initial settlement offers
  • Persistent underpayment after dispute
  • Failure to respond to valuation disputes
  • Need to formally document settlement position
  • Pre-litigation requirement in some jurisdictions
  • Establishing clear record of policyholder position

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Do not make demands without factual support or documentation
  • Do not threaten litigation or legal action without attorney consultation
  • Do not demand unreasonable amounts beyond documented losses
  • Do not use hostile or threatening language
  • Do not set unrealistic or arbitrary response deadlines

What to Do Next

Calculate specific settlement amount based on documented losses, replacement costs, and policy coverage. Compile all supporting documentation including estimates, receipts, appraisals, and policy provisions. Draft demand letter stating specific amount, factual basis, and reasonable response timeline.

Send demand via certified mail with return receipt. If demand involves significant amounts or complex legal issues, consult an attorney before proceeding. For straightforward valuation-based demands, you may prepare correspondence using this system.
